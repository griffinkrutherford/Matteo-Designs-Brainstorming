Historic Adobe Renovation

Transforming a 1920s Santa Fe adobe home into a sustainable, modern living space while preserving its historic character and architectural integrity. This LEED certified renovation demonstrates how historic preservation and modern sustainability can coexist beautifully.

The Challenge & Our Solution

The Challenge

The 100-year-old adobe home had significant structural issues, poor insulation, outdated systems, and a closed-off floor plan that didn't suit modern living. The clients wanted to preserve the home's historic character while achieving LEED certification and dramatically improving energy efficiency‚Äîa seemingly contradictory goal.

  • Deteriorating adobe walls requiring stabilization
  • Single-pane windows losing significant heat
  • Inefficient heating system
  • Dark, closed-off rooms
  • Historic District restrictions on exterior changes

Our Solution

We developed a comprehensive renovation strategy that respected the home's heritage while incorporating modern sustainable technologies. By working closely with historic preservation authorities and using traditional materials in innovative ways, we achieved both goals.

  • Restored adobe walls with traditional mud plaster
  • Installed custom wood windows matching historic profiles
  • Added radiant floor heating using solar thermal
  • Created open flow while maintaining traditional vigas
  • Achieved LEED Gold certification
